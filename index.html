Presentation Reflection

This presentation was a great way to practice the public speaking skills we will need later in our careers. Overall, the group performed well in the presentation, and we delivered some asthetically pleasing slides to accompany us.

In the future, I would like to work more with the group to practice the transitions and speaking order. I would also like to work more on the physical aspects of presenting, such as: voie infleciton, hand motions, and eye contact.

Overall, this project was a good experience that taught us lessons about world health issues and public speaking. Not only were we able to practice public speaking with freedom, but we were also able to enrich our knowledge of the medical crisis today.

Quinncia

The Quinncia Resume Review and Interview served as a perfect resource for job readiness.

Nowadays, nearly every resume is read by artificial intelligence, and it is crucial that your resume gets processed rather than rejected by these algorithms.

Companies also use artificial intelligence for preliminary interviews using programs such as HireVue.

The Quinncia practice Interview gave crucial feedback, such as slowing down speech, taking time to process the question, and using key words and phrases to demonstrate credibility.

Online Interview

This was the first AI interview I ever took after doing the Quinncia mock interview. This was an excellent process to practice for interviews, and learn what the AI would most likely ask of you. Although it can be a bit frustrating talking to an AI computer program, it is still a good learning example for when you have a face to face interview at some point.

Working With a Group Remotely

Our group primarily used remote contact to finish the project. During periods of class, we would schedule times where we would meet online to discuss and work on the project. During this period on zoom, we also issued out new tasks and ideas for each member to increase offline productivity. We rehearsed the presentation on zoom, and planned for the final presentation without ever having met in person to rehearse. While this may not be entirely optimal, we managed to be efficient and delivered a solid presentation in the end.
